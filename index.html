About NAMAMI GANGA PROJECT

Namami Ganga Project: Revitalizing India's Lifeline

The Namami Ganga Project, also known as the Clean Ganga Mission, is an ambitious initiative launched by the Government of India in 2014 to restore the sanctity and purity of the Ganga River. The project aims to rejuvenate the river's health, improve its water quality, and promote sustainable development along its banks.

Objectives:

1. Improve water quality by reducing pollution from industrial, agricultural, and domestic sources.
2. Restore ecological balance and biodiversity.
3. Promote sustainable livelihoods and economic development.
4. Enhance public awareness and community participation.

Components:

1. Pollution Abatement: Constructing sewage treatment plants, industrial effluent treatment plants, and crematoria.
2. Riverfront Development: Creating parks, ghats, and walkways to enhance aesthetic appeal.
3. Afforestation: Planting trees and restoring wetlands to maintain ecological balance.
4. Capacity Building: Training stakeholders, including local communities and government officials.
5. Public-Private Partnerships: Collaborating with NGOs, corporate entities, and international organizations.

Achievements:

1. Sewage Treatment Capacity: Increased from 190 MLD (million liters per day) to 1,200 MLD.
2. Industrial Effluent Treatment: 150 industrial units have been closed or relocated.
3. Riverfront Development: 100+ ghats and 50+ parks constructed.
4. Afforestation: 2 million trees planted.
5. Community Engagement: 1,500+ gram sabhas (village councils) involved.

Conclusion:

The Namami Ganga Project is a significant step towards revitalizing India's iconic river. While challenges persist, the project's achievements demonstrate progress. Sustained efforts, innovative solutions, and community engagement will be crucial in achieving the project's vision of a clean, healthy, and thriving Ganga River.

Projects for Cleaning GANGA

Clean Ganga Fund (CGF)

A program that allows corporations to participate in the Ganga's rejuvenation as part of their Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) activities. Companies can choose project activities and develop proposals to execute them. Domestic donors to CGF are eligible for 100% income tax exemption.

World Bank assistance

The World Bank has provided technical assistance and financing to the National Ganga River Basin Authority (NGRBA) to help reduce pollution.

Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) support

JICA has supported a project in Varanasi worth Rs. 496.9 Crore
